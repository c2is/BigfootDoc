1801: punched card technology

Joseph-Marie Jacquard

  • In 1801, Joseph-Marie Jacquard developed a loom in which the pattern being woven was controlled by punched cards.

Charles Babbage

  • In 1833, Charles Babbage moved on from developing his difference engine (for navigational calculations) to a general purpose design, the Analytical Engine, which drew directly on Jacquard's punched cards for its program storage.2
  • In 1837, Babbage described his analytical engine. It was a general-purpose programmable computer, employing punch cards for input and a steam engine for power, using the positions of gears and shafts to represent numbers.3
